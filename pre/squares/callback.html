.title Callbacks and flourishes

.desc {{ Styling is anything done in response to a call that is not part of the call's definition. }} This might be an extra verbal or physical action, or it might be a different way of dancing the call that still ends up in the same place.

Many members of Tech Squares enthusiastically adopt many styling elements common throughout the square dancing world. But we've invented some new ones, too.

Thanks to CJ Quines for compiling an initial version of these lists.

callbacks (sound effects)

Communication between the caller and the dancers in square dancing is not one-way -- the caller is usually the one saying things to the dancers, but sometimes the dancers say things back. These are referred to as "callbacks" at Tech Squares, though I believe the more standard term is "sound effects".

The IAGSDC History wiki also documents some of the more widespread sound effects, which overlaps partially with this list, but for the most part these are largely confined to Tech Squares.

  • @allemande left@: one clap
    • According to Tech Squares caller Ted Lizotte, "If you step in a supermarket and say Allemande Left, you'll hear the square dancers clap." In practice, this is only common at a handful of clubs.
  • @couples trade@: "trade, trade, trade..."
    • This is repeated until the call ends. It is sometimes extended to other as-couples calls, like @couples hinge@.
  • @left swing thru@: "left?!"
    • This alerts dancers to when the less common handedness is used. The same callback, or "right?!", is also heard for other calls with handedness, like veers, leads, circles, etc.
  • @lines forward and back@: "thru!"
    • This is a joke, referring to the call @lines (anything) thru@.
  • @wheel and deal@: "wheeling!" or "dealing!" from lines back-to-back
    • The belle couple is wheeling (because it feels like a wheel around), and the beau couple is dealing (with the other two dancers trying to get in front of them).
  • @chain down the line@: centers say "catch me, turn me!"
  • @ladies center, men sashay@: as eloquently phrased by the IAGSDC wiki, "ladies whoop it up"
  • @u-turn back@: "u-turn back?!"
  • @all 8 circulate@: "all circ-eightulate!"
    • Other circulates are also spoonerized, like "circ splitulate", "circum colulate", "circond dimulate", etc. As a special case, @couples circulate@ is "like bunnies!" (often while hopping up and down), referring to the would-be spoonerism "circles copulate".
  • @zoom@: there are several:
    • "0-2-1-3-4" referencing an old TV show

    • "you're on mute!" post-pandemic referencing the videoconferencing platform
