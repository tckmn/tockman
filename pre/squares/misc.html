jokes

  • By analogy with {switch to a diamond} and {switch to an hourglass}, {ends cut in} could be described as "switch to a crossover"... and {trade} is "switch to a split".
  • {plus|explode} shouldn't be a plus call. Based on its name, it should be C4.

anecdotes

  • There was a pool dance I participated in at a squares weekend (it's exactly what it sounds like, just a square dance but in a pool. We did one on an ice rink too). My favorite moment was "pass to the cen- wait, no, dive thru!!"
  • The frozen peas anecdote is documented in a few places on the internet; however, it's been extended even further at Tech Squares. Our callback is "Kevin's peas are frozen!", because a member named Kevin was the one who first introduced it here. Apparently, even knowledge of this one has spread; at IAGSDC 2022, when Sandie Bryant called a mini-busy in a hall with enough Tech Squares dancers, she was ready to retort to our "Kevin's peas are frozen!" with an immediate and deadpan "...they are not".

observations

  • From an eight chain, "circulate once and a half" is the same as "circulate a half, circulate a half".
  • From facing lines, 1/2 stable regroup = hocus pocus.
  • There's an obvious generalization to define zip code 0: centers 1/4 out and jog (that's either cast back or press out).
  • The word "spin" has many meanings:
    • swing, e.g. {spin the top} vs {C3A|the top}
    • swing slip, e.g. {spin the windmill} vs {the windmill}
    • hinge by 1/2 x 3/4, e.g. {spin chain thru} vs {C1|chain thru}
    • left touch 3/4, e.g. {rotary spin} vs {rotary}
    • right touch 3/4, e.g. {spin the pulley} vs {the pulley}
